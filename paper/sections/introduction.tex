\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

FunSearch~\cite{fawzi2024funsearch} showed that LLMs can discover new
mathematical constructions by evolving programs.
AlphaEvolve~\cite{deepmind2025alphaevolve} scaled this to algorithm
design across mathematics and computer science. But evaluating what
LLM agents \emph{actually discover}---as opposed to what they
overfit---remains an open problem. Existing benchmarks for
LLM-generated optimization code (HeuriGym~\cite{yao2025heurigym},
CO-Bench~\cite{cobench2025}) evaluate on same-distribution instances,
making it impossible to distinguish genuine algorithmic insight from
hyperparameter tuning to a specific problem.

We present FunWake, a benchmark with three properties:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Minimal interface.} The LLM writes a single function
    with 4 inputs and 4 outputs:
    \texttt{schedule\_fn(step, total\_steps, lr0, alpha0)} $\to$
    \texttt{(lr, alpha, beta1, beta2)}. A fixed skeleton handles
    initialization, gradients, and Adam updates. Each evaluation takes
    30--60 seconds on CPU.
    \item \textbf{Physics-based held-out evaluation.} The held-out
    farm differs from training in turbine model, polygon geometry,
    turbine count, and wind resource. The LLM never sees held-out AEP.
    Additionally, the training farm is evaluated at seven turbine
    counts ($N=30$--$100$) to test scaling generalization.
    \item \textbf{Low cost.} Evaluating a submitted schedule is free
    (CPU only). Running an LLM agent through the full benchmark costs
    \$5--50 depending on model. Full paper reproduction costs
    $\sim$\$500.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{What the agents discover.}
Across four models and 700+ generated schedules, two qualitatively
novel schedule structures emerge:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Dual Gaussian LR bumps} (Claude Code): brief
    learning rate increases at $t=0.5$ and $t=0.75$ with a coordinated
    penalty dip at $t=0.6$---controlled escapes from local optima
    followed by constraint re-enforcement. Held-out: +24.8~GWh.
    \item \textbf{Decaying sinusoidal shake} (Gemini CLI): 80-cycle
    high-frequency perturbation with $\sqrt{1-t}$ amplitude decay,
    paired with a 7500$\times$ quadratic penalty ramp and zero first
    momentum ($\beta_1 = 0$). Held-out: +81.3~GWh.
\end{itemize}

Neither structure appears in the learning rate schedule
literature~\cite{loshchilov2017sgdr}. Standard schedules (cosine
decay, exponential decay, warm restarts) decay monotonically;
these schedules inject structured noise that decays over time.
The penalty coupling ($\alpha \propto 1/\eta$ or $\alpha \propto t^2$)
is critical: schedules that reduce penalty weight at convergence
produce feasible layouts on the spacious training farm but fail on the
tighter held-out polygon.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
(1)~An open-source benchmark for LLM-discovered optimizer schedules
with physics-based held-out evaluation and scalable difficulty.
(2)~Documentation of two novel schedule structures discovered
autonomously by LLM agents, with analysis of why they generalize.
(3)~A generalization study across seven farm sizes showing that
LLM-discovered schedules maintain feasibility where the baseline
fails.
