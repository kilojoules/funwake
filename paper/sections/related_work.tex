\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{LLMs as algorithm designers.}
FunSearch~\cite{fawzi2024funsearch} evolves programs with an LLM
to discover mathematical constructions;
AlphaEvolve~\cite{deepmind2025alphaevolve} scales this across
matrix multiplication, scheduling, and geometry;
ReEvo~\cite{ye2024reevo}, EoH~\cite{liu2024eoh}, and
LLaMEA~\cite{llamea2024} apply related ideas to combinatorial
optimization and metaheuristics. These systems evaluate generated
code on same-distribution instances; Sim et
al.~\cite{sim2025beyondhype} show FunSearch heuristics for bin
packing do not generalize to broader suites. FunWake adopts the
FunSearch pattern (LLM fills a function signature, fixed harness)
but adds physics-based held-out evaluation.

\paragraph{LLMs inside optimization loops.}
OPRO~\cite{yang2024opro} uses LLMs to optimize from natural
language descriptions of past performance, with the LLM in the
inner loop. FunWake differs in that the LLM writes a schedule
function \emph{offline}: the 8000-step optimization runs without
any per-step LLM involvement, enabling cheap CPU-only evaluation.

\paragraph{Automated schedule discovery.}
Population-Based Training~\cite{jaderberg2017pbt} jointly evolves a
population of training schedules during optimization;
BOHB~\cite{falkner2018bohb} combines Bayesian optimization with
bandit-based early stopping for hyperparameter search. FunWake's
random-search and DE ablations serve a similar purpose---searching
inside a parameterized schedule family---but the schedule space is
much smaller (4 scalars per step), and the LLM's contribution is
proposing the parameterization itself rather than searching within a
known one.

\paragraph{Agentic coding benchmarks.}
HeuriGym~\cite{yao2025heurigym} evaluates LLM-generated heuristics
on discrete combinatorial problems with same-distribution held-out
instances. FunWake evaluates on a continuous constrained
optimization problem with \emph{physically distinct} held-out
instances (different turbine model, polygon, wind resource).
Scaffold differences (rich CLI agents vs.\ structured-output
prompting) also confound cross-model comparisons, motivating our
use of a single portable scaffold for the open-source arm.

\paragraph{Wind farm layout optimization.}
The WFLOP places $N$ turbines to maximize energy production subject
to boundary and spacing
constraints~\cite{mosetti1994optimization,grady2005placement};
gradient methods exploit differentiable wake
models~\cite{thomas2023improving,quick2024design,stanley2019massive};
evolutionary methods handle multimodality through population
diversity~\cite{chen2013wind,rios2023population}.
TopFarm~\cite{pedersen2019topfarm} provides the SGD solver whose
internal schedule FunWake's agents aim to improve. No prior work
applies LLMs to wind farm layout optimization.
