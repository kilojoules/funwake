\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Main Comparison}
\label{sec:main_comparison}

We evaluate four LLM agents on the schedule-only interface, each with a
5-hour wall-clock budget and the same fixed skeleton. The 500-multi-start
SGD baseline (best-known non-LLM result) achieves 4246.7\,GWh on the
held-out ROWP farm. Figure~\ref{fig:rowp} shows the best held-out AEP
for each model.

\subsection{Training vs Held-Out Divergence}
\label{sec:divergence}

\textbf{The training-optimal schedule is not the held-out-optimal
schedule, and infeasibility is the dominant failure mode.} Several
agents produced schedules with training AEP at the practical ceiling
(5600.0\,GWh) that scored higher than 4272\,GWh on ROWP---but these
layouts violate boundary or spacing constraints. When restricted to
\emph{feasible} held-out layouts only, the best Gemini and Claude
schedules both score $\sim$4272\,GWh. Training AEP at the ceiling does
not distinguish feasible from infeasible generalizers.

\textbf{Training AEP has a ceiling near 5600\,GWh.} Both frontier
models hit 5600.0 exactly on multiple attempts, suggesting the training
landscape has a practically reachable local optimum. Qwen and Llama
full-optimizer runs max out at 5547--5549\,GWh, below this ceiling.

globally-best ROWP seen during the run, reflecting that selection on
training AEP alone is suboptimal.

\subsection{Discovered Schedule Structures}
\label{sec:structures}

Figure~\ref{fig:schedules} visualizes the four schedule parameters
over optimization progress for the three best schedules: the baseline,
Claude's iter 192 (dual Gaussian bumps + alpha dip), and Gemini's
iter 067 (decaying sinusoidal shake + quadratic alpha ramp).

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figs/fig3_top3_schedules.png}
\caption{Schedule parameters over optimization progress for the
baseline (TopFarm-style monotonic), Claude's iter 192 (dual Gaussian
LR bumps at $t=0.5, 0.75$ with penalty dip at $t=0.6$), and Gemini's
iter 067 (80-cycle sinusoidal shake with $\sqrt{1-t}$ amplitude decay;
$\beta_1=0$; quadratic alpha ramp to $7500\times$). Note the log-scale
y-axis on $\alpha$: Gemini's penalty weight reaches two orders of
magnitude higher than the baseline.}
\label{fig:schedules}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{Claude's dual Gaussian bumps.}
The learning rate is $4\eta_0 \cdot \texttt{cosine}(t)$ with Gaussian
bumps at $t=0.5$ and $t=0.75$ (+20\% and +30\% of initial rate).
Penalty weight grows as $\alpha_\text{base}(t) \cdot (1 - 0.5\cdot
\exp(-(t-0.6)^2/0.0032))$, with a dip at $t=0.6$ between the bumps.
$\beta_1 = 0.3$, $\beta_2 = 0.5$. The intuition: bumps escape local
optima, the penalty dip permits rearrangement during the second bump,
and moderate momentum damps post-bump oscillations.

\paragraph{Gemini's sinusoidal shake.}
The learning rate is $\eta_\text{cosine}(t) \cdot (1 + 0.08\sqrt{1-t}
\cdot \sin(80\pi t))$---a high-frequency perturbation (80 cycles over
the run) with $\sqrt{1-t}$ amplitude decay. The penalty weight is
$\alpha_0 (1 + 7499 t^2)$, reaching 7500$\times$ by the final
iteration (the baseline reaches $\sim$100$\times$). $\beta_1 = 0$
(no momentum). The intuition: continuous shake prevents the optimizer
from settling into a local minimum early; extreme late-stage penalty
forces feasibility regardless of how the schedule approaches convergence.

\paragraph{Both schedules generalize because they maintain high
penalty at convergence.} On the spacious training farm, even weak
constraint handling produces feasible layouts. On the tighter ROWP
farm, only schedules with high $\alpha$ in the final iterations
produce feasible layouts. Every schedule that fails on ROWP has
$\alpha$ decreasing or plateauing at the end.

\subsection{Full-Optimizer Mode}
\label{sec:results_full}

For completeness, we also run the 4 models in full-optimizer mode
(writing an arbitrary \texttt{optimize()} function). All four models
converge to the same strategy: wrap \texttt{topfarm\_sgd\_solve} with
a better initialization (hexagonal lattice, K-means clustering,
wind-direction-aware placement) and tuned hyperparameters (higher
learning rate, standard Adam momentum). No model produces a custom
gradient optimizer that passes held-out feasibility. Full-optimizer
held-out AEP: Claude +21.8, Llama +5.3, Qwen +4.7. The schedule-only
interface produces better held-out results than the full-optimizer
interface for Claude (+24.8 vs +21.8), suggesting that constraining
the search space to the schedule-only interface forces deeper
exploration of optimization dynamics.
