\begin{abstract}
We present an evaluation framework for testing whether LLM agents can
autonomously design optimization algorithms for real engineering problems.
An LLM receives a wake simulation API, a training farm, and a time
budget. It writes optimizer functions evaluated on the training farm,
with silent scoring on a held-out farm with a different turbine, polygon,
and wind resource. Across runs with Gemini~2.5~Flash, we find that LLMs
consistently discover effective optimization \emph{strategies} ---
wind-direction-aware initialization, two-stage feasibility-then-objective
optimization, diverse multi-start --- but never produce novel optimization
\emph{algorithms}. All winning solutions wrap the existing gradient solver
with tuned hyperparameters. Custom gradient implementations fail constraint
checks on the held-out farm. A stressed-polygon unit test predicts
held-out failure with high accuracy.
\end{abstract}
